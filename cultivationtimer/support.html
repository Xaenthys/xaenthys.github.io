Getting Started

Cultivation Timer is a focused practice timer for meditation, breathwork, internal arts, and other contemplative practices. It supports multi-phase timing, saved exercises, practice sets, session history, practice targets, and consistency tracking.

The core timer is free, with no time limits, no ads, and no account required. The free plan lets you save up to 3 exercises and 1 practice set, and shows your last 7 days of practice history in the Day and Week statistics views. Cultivation Timer Plus unlocks unlimited exercises, unlimited practice sets, your full practice history with Month and Year statistics views, and practice targets.

Open the Library tab, choose Exercises, and tap the + button. Give the exercise a name, set its duration, add an optional preparation countdown and interval chimes, choose its sounds, then save. The exercise appears in your Library, ready to load into the Timer.

Open the Library tab, choose Practice Sets, and tap +. Add timed exercises in the order you want to practice them, with an optional preparation countdown before the first exercise and a rest between exercises. When you run a practice set, the timer moves through each exercise automatically.

On the Timer tab, tap the title at the top to choose an exercise or practice set from your Library — or simply set a duration and start a quick session. You can also use “Load in Timer” on any exercise or practice set in the Library. The timer guides you through each phase and plays your chosen sounds at each transition.

These are the optional phases you can add around your main practice time. Preparation is a short countdown to settle in before practice begins. Intervals play a chime at a steady rhythm during practice — for example, every 5 minutes. Extended Practice keeps the timer counting after time is up, so you can keep going and still record your full session. You can also make an exercise Open-Ended to practice without a time limit; note that open-ended exercises cannot be used in practice sets.

Statistics & Targets

Practice targets are a Plus feature. Make sure Practice Targets is enabled in Settings, then open the Stats tab and tap the target area on the summary card to set or edit the target for the period you’re viewing. Each timescale — day, week, month, and year — has its own target. Removing a target keeps your past target history saved.

Consistency shows how regularly you practice: the percentage of days in the period on which you practiced, alongside the day count (for example, “5 of 7 days”). It appears in the Week, Month, and Year statistics views. You can show or hide it with the Practice Consistency toggle in Settings.

Yes. In the Stats tab, tap the + button to log a session manually. Choose one of your exercises — or “General practice” with an optional label — then set the duration and start time and save. Manual sessions count toward your history, targets, and consistency just like timed sessions.

Subscriptions & Purchases

  • Unlimited saved exercises (free plan: 3)
  • Unlimited practice sets (free plan: 1)
  • Full practice history (free plan: last 7 days)
  • Month and Year statistics views
  • Practice targets for day, week, month, and year

Monthly, Yearly, and Lifetime (one-time purchase) options are available. All unlock the same Plus features. Current pricing — and any free trial offer — is shown in the app.

Nothing is deleted. If you have more saved than the free plan allows, the app asks you to choose which exercises (up to 3) and which practice set to keep. The rest are hidden — not deleted — and return automatically if you upgrade again. If a slot opens up later, you can also restore a hidden exercise from the Library.

Open Settings, choose Cultivation Timer Plus, then tap “Restore Purchases” on the Plus screen. Your purchase will be verified with the App Store and restored automatically. Make sure you’re signed into the same Apple ID used for the original purchase.

Subscriptions are managed through Apple. On your iPhone, go to Settings → [Your Name] → Subscriptions, find Cultivation Timer, and tap Cancel Subscription. You’ll retain Plus access until the end of the current billing period.

Refunds are handled by Apple. Visit reportaproblem.apple.com to request a refund for any App Store purchase.

Troubleshooting

Timer chimes play through your media volume and are not silenced by the ring/silent switch, so practice cues still sound when your phone is muted. If you don’t hear chimes: check your media volume (use the volume buttons while the timer is running), check the per-sound volumes under Settings → Sound Defaults and in the exercise itself, and make sure audio isn’t routing to a paired Bluetooth device. If audio from another app competes with your chimes, turn on “Lower Other Audio” in Settings to quiet other apps while the timer runs.

Open Settings and check “Screen During Practice.” Choose “Stay Awake” to keep the screen on while the timer is running, or “Dim to Black” to keep the timer active while dimming the display. Either way, the timer keeps running and chimes still play if the screen does turn off.

Open Settings, choose Cultivation Timer Plus, then tap “Restore Purchases” on the Plus screen. If the issue continues, confirm you’re signed into the correct Apple ID and that your subscription or lifetime purchase is still active. If it still doesn’t resolve, contact us directly.

The free plan supports up to 3 saved exercises. You can delete an exercise to make room — or, if you have hidden exercises from an earlier Plus plan, restore one into an open slot from the Library. To save unlimited exercises, upgrade to Plus from the prompt in the app.

Privacy & Data

Your practice data — exercises, practice sets, session history, targets, and settings — is stored locally on your device. The app does not send this practice data to our servers or third-party analytics or advertising services. If you contact support, your email and any included diagnostics are sent to the support address. See our Privacy Policy for full details.

Deleting the app removes all locally stored data permanently. There is currently no cloud backup or sync. If you want to preserve your data, do not delete the app. Standard iOS device backups (iCloud or Finder) will include your app data.

Open Settings → Data & Privacy → Reset Statistics. This permanently deletes your practice history, target history, and history kept from deleted exercises — it cannot be undone. Your exercises, practice sets, and settings stay in place.

No. Cultivation Timer contains no analytics SDKs, no advertising frameworks, and no third-party tracking of any kind. See our Privacy Policy for the full picture.
